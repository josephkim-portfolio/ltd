Career Reflections

PROGRAM REFLECTIONS

I began my program as a part-time student in Fall 2016 with the goal of changing my career. Having worked in higher education for a number of years in various roles, I learned of a role that combined analysis, research, and leveraging best practices from the learning sciences to craft optimized learning experiences. Curiosity led to commitment, and I’m happy that I was privileged with the opportunity to switch into this career while pursuing studies at the University of Missouri.

In reflection, the program of study blended well competencies in analysis and project management, an array of design courses, and curriculum/pedagogical best practices. My learnings provided foundational knowledge in curriculum development, design, and in authoring tools. I appreciated most the balance between theory and practical skills to design, author, and create. The program’s portfolio and artifact culmination evidences this balance of foundational theory and practical, career-relevant deliverables.The endeavor of crafting meaningful learning experiences is a genuine love of mine, and my learnings throughout the Learning Technologies and Design program developed my knowledge and skillset in the field of instructional design.

PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT PLAN

My experiences in Instructional Design has mainly been in Higher Education academic contexts. I am interested in developing curriculum in the context of workplace Learning and Development. Additionally, I aim to broaden and deepen design and authoring skills in applications relevant to the field. Below entail my next professional development goals:

  • Competency Based Education - My LTD program emphasized the importance of well-defined learning outcomes and acceptable evidences (conveyed by the Understanding by Design approach). A outcomes-oriented model that is growing in popularity in K-12 and Higher Education settings is the Competency Based Education (CBE) model. I am interested in learning how a CBE model can apply in Higher Education and Learning and Development workplace learning contexts.
  • Articulate Storyline - Learning Adobe Captivate in my program was extremely practical and rewarding. An alternative authoring tool, Articulate Storyline, is a popular option with robust design features that I would like to learn and compare with Adobe Captivate.
  • Articulate Rise 360 - As part of the Articulate suite, Rise 360 is a web-based authoring tool that allows rapid-development of learning solutions, as it is template-based. Additionally, the platform highly values accessible features in its design, which is extremely needed for equitable learning in Higher Education and workplace settings.
  • Adobe Illustrator - At the end of my program, I find that graphic design skills is relevant to a variety of tasks for an Instructional Designer. I aim to learn Adobe Illustrator to utilize and manipulate visual character and objects to create scenarios for scenario-based e-learning.
